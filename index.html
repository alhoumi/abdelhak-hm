About

A Young Man Who
Lived Honestly

"I am not a writer, an intellectual, or a novelist. I simply love to write."

Abdo HM is a young Moroccan writer from Casablanca — a city he wants to escape but that never escapes him. He writes not for fame or recognition, but because writing is his only way to not disappear in silence.

His work is raw, unfiltered, and deeply human. Born from lived experience, his words speak to everyone who has ever felt that life is a masquerade party they are attending without a mask.

When not writing, he travels — seeking the small seas that no one visits, the northern breakfasts that taste of something real, and the stars you can only count when sleeping on Bedouin mats far from city lights.

Book I — 2024
حياة لا تستحق العيش
A Life Not Worth Living

A very simple book, rich with dark events. A farewell letter and a gift to the people he loves. An honest account of a young man living through Casablanca's harsh streets, broken relationships, solitude, and the relentless question of whether it is all worth it.

Written in both Arabic and English, this debut work is not a manifesto of despair — it is an act of survival through honesty.

"I did not write this book to convince you that life is not worth living, nor to prove that it is. I wrote it only to say: this is how I lived it."

— Abdo HM, The End

Book II — 2026
ضوء ما بعد المسودة
Light After the Draft

The second book by Abdelhak El Haoumy — a raw, intimate journey through darkness, roots, wounds, faith, and the fragile light that survives. Written in Darija and Arabic, it is not a story of salvation, but of someone who kept going without knowing why.

Five chapters. Five parts of a soul laid bare: the draft, the roots, the wound and the gold, God and the dark, and finally — the light.

"ضو قليل. ولكن راه كان تمّا."

— Abdo HM  ·  A little light. But it was there.
